# Ashen Banner — Story Bible

> *The old kingdom burned. All that's left of it is a scorched banner, and five
> who still carry it.*

## Premise

Thirty years ago the realm of **Calenmark** was a kingdom. Then the fires came,
and **Maldrath the Unbowed** — once Calenmark's own Lord-Marshal — turned his
armies on the crown he was sworn to. The capital fell in a single night. The
royal house of **Calen** was put to the sword, its halls gutted, its standard
left burning in the gatehouse.

But the banner did not burn through. Scorched grey, edges eaten to ash, it
survived — and so did a handful of those who'd sworn to it. They are not an army.
They are five. They carry the **Ashen Banner** from the border wilds to
Maldrath's shattered keep, raising it over every field the tyrant thought he had
already won.

This is a campaign about an underdog resistance: small, outnumbered, and
unwilling to kneel. Each battle is a banner planted somewhere it isn't supposed
to be.

## The world

**Calenmark** is a broken country — cinder-fields where farmland used to be,
hill passes held by Maldrath's freebooters, river chasms spanned by a single
stone bridge, and at its heart the old royal keep, now half-collapsed and ringed
by two black chasms. The realm is medieval-fantasy in shape: knights and archers,
fire-mages and battle-clerics, monks who fight bare-fisted.

Magic is real but disciplined — it answers to study (Black Mage), to faith
(White Mage), and to the body's own discipline (Monk). There are no gods on the
field, only people who learned to channel fire, mend wounds, or break stone with
a fist.

## The Ashen Banner — the five

The party. Five fixed characters, each a class with a distinct role. They begin
seasoned, not green (level 3) — survivors, not recruits.

| Name | Class | Who they are |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **Garan** | Knight | Last of House Calen's royal guard. He carries the banner and holds the line; where Garan stands, the others rally. |
| **Lyra** | Archer | A border scout who knew the hills before Maldrath did. Eyes of the band; she shoots first and is rarely wrong. |
| **Vex** | Black Mage | A scholar of fire and storm who lost a library to the burning. Now he aims what he learned at the people who lit the match. |
| **Mira** | White Mage | A battle-cleric of the old faith. She mends, she shields, and when the worst happens, she raises the fallen back to their feet. |
| **Bron** | Monk | A wandering brawler who needed no reason to hate Maldrath and found five anyway. Fists, discipline, and an unreasonable amount of HP. |

> In-game, any character can be retrained into any class at the Party Camp, so the
> five above are starting roles, not cages. The story treats them by their
> original calling.

## The tyrant and his court

**Maldrath the Unbowed** — the final boss. A war-marshal who never knelt to the
crown and never will to anything else. He fights from the throne of the keep he
sacked, flanked by the hand-picked guard who kept him there: **Seris**, his
throne-cleric; **Vexin the Emberborn**, a fire-mage to mirror Vex; **Ironsworn
Hale**; **Bastion Monk Garr**; and **Throne Archer Vael**.

Between the border and the throne stand his **Sorcerer's Court** — the cabal that
props the regime up: the pyromancer **Velis**, the stormcaller **Dane**, the
fallen abbess **Mirelle**, and their guards. Break the court and the throne has
nothing left to hide behind.

## The campaign — seven chapters

The game ships as **seven phases**, played in order, with a Party Camp between
each for class changes, gear, skill (JP) spending, and — as the war widens —
reinforcements: the party starts at four and earns extra deployment slots
mid-campaign and for the finale. Progress saves automatically.

### Chapter I — Tutorial Skirmish
*A grassy field, a brigand camp, and the first lesson of the war.*

The band crosses Maldrath's border and runs straight into his freebooters —
**Brigand Swordsman**, **Camp Lookout**, **Wandering Brawler**. Flat ground, a
fair fight, and the place the player learns to move, strike, and end a turn. The
Ashen Banner flies for the first time in thirty years.

### Chapter II — Ambush in the Hills
*Brigands on the high ground, and a sorcerer weaving flame between the ridges.*

The hills belong to the enemy, who hold the elevated ground and screen a **Hill
Sorcerer** behind ridge sentries and brawlers. The lesson here is terrain:
height, jump, and the cost of charging uphill into a mage.

### Chapter III — The Bridge
*A river cleaves the valley; one narrow stone bridge is the only way across.*

A choke point. Maldrath's **Bridge Vanguard**, **River Guard**, a **Far Bank
Archer** and a **Cliffside Mage** hold the far side of a chasm crossed by a
single span. Funnel, hold, and break through — or be picked apart on the
approach.

### Chapter IV — The Cinder Fields
*A wide plain of burned farmland; nowhere to hide and a company that means to surround you.*

Past the bridge the land opens into black stubble and broken fences — a
larger, open battlefield with no chokepoint to hold. A roving company under a
**Cinder Marauder** spreads to flank: an **Ash Archer** and **Scorch Mage** at
range, a **Field Cleric** keeping them up, a **Brigand Lieutenant** circling for
the back. The lesson is space: hold the rubble for cover, watch your facing, and
don't get enveloped on open ground.

### Chapter V — Sorcerer's Court
*Tiered stone terraces, a stack of mages, a healer who won't let them die.*

The inner court that keeps the regime standing: **Pyromancer Velis**,
**Stormcaller Dane**, the abbess **Mirelle**, and their guards on raised
terraces, raining AoE and mending each other. A war of attrition against a team
that fights like one.

### Chapter VI — The Outer Ramparts
*The keep's fortified wall: merlons, archers on the high stone, and a single gate.*

Before the throne stands the outer rampart — a raised stone wall ringing a
courtyard killing-floor. A **Rampart Captain** holds the gate while a **Siege
Mage**, two wall sentries, and a **Gate Cleric** fire down from the high stone.
Force the gate or scale the ends, but the longer you stand in the open, the more
the wall whittles you down — line of sight and the high ground are the whole
fight here.

### Chapter VII — The Tyrant's Stand
*A shattered keep, two chasms, and the throne at the end of it all.*

**Maldrath the Unbowed** and his throne-guard make their last stand on a broken
keep ringed by chasms, with a single narrow approach to the killing floor. The
hardest fight in the game and the end of the war: sunder the throne, or fall
unbowed beside the banner.

## Tone & themes

- **Underdog resistance.** Five against a regime. Every win is taken, never given.
- **What survives the fire.** The banner is the through-line: scorched, never
  burned, raised again and again. The realm can be broken and the people in it
  still refuse to bow.
- **No chosen ones.** The five are guards, scouts, scholars, clerics, and
  brawlers — ordinary survivors who happened not to quit.

## Where the story lives in the build today

The narrative above is the intended frame. In the current build it surfaces as:
map names, the one-line **intro text** on each phase, and the named enemies and
party members. Full in-engine dialogue and cutscenes are on the
[roadmap](ROADMAP.md), not yet in the game — the story bible is the source of
truth they'll be written from.
